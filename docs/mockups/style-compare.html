Pedalboard UI — art-direction studies

Three competing styles of the same three screens (Pedalboard · Pedal editor · Macros), same data, same interactions — pick a direction. Research behind them: docs/PLUGIN_UI_RESEARCH.md.

Style A — Refined Hybrid research lean

Believable pedal enclosures in their hardware-archetype colors (TS green, CE-2 blue, blackface cream, Carbon-Copy green) on quiet flat-dark chrome. Soundtoys/UAD school — depth without screws or wood.

Style B — Flat Modern

Near-black canvas, thin-line arc knobs, one vivid accent hue per pedal, live motion driven by the real parameter values. Vital/Strymon-plugin/FabFilter school.

Style C — Hardware Authentic

Full skeuomorphism: pedals with screws, knurled knobs, and silk-screen type sitting on a textured board joined by sagging patch cables; macros as a brushed-aluminum rack unit. Cherry Audio/AmpliTube school.

Baseline — v1 dashboard style for contrast

The original plain mockup in the current app's design language (docs/PEDALBOARD_UI.md). Kept as the control sample.

All files are self-contained — open them straight from disk. Knobs in the editor/macros screens respond to vertical drag and double-click-to-reset in every style, so the comparison covers feel, not just looks.
